# Policy-Enforced Execution (agentic design pattern)

**Policy-Enforced Execution** is an agentic *design pattern*: a reusable way to **block or condition actions** against
explicit **trust and ethics rules**—labor, sanctions, sustainability—so “the model wanted to” never overrides what the
company refuses to sign—before you pick a concrete product story (auction, hiring, logistics, etc.).

In this layout, **policy decision points** sit on workflow transitions and tool paths; identity-attributed facts feed
those checks, and denials return explainable rule codes—not silent failures.

Coffee Agntcy simply will not **contract** with partners that violate the policies it publishes; automation must inherit
that spine.
